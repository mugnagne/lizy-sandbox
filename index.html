Classic Leasing vs Circular Leasing

Single usage

Often used by one driver before resale.

In traditional leasing, vehicles are typically used by one person and quickly resold. This limits the potential lifespan of the car and leads to increased production and waste.

Minimal maintenance

Only the basics are covered.

Minimal servicing increases the risk of mechanical failures and accelerates depreciation, resulting in a shorter usable life and greater environmental strain.

Poor end-of-life handling

Sold quickly without reconditioning.

Cars are rarely refurbished or properly recycled. This approach promotes a linear economy, where products are discarded instead of reused or repurposed.

Multi-user lifecycle

Vehicle is reused across several leases.

Instead of a single-use cycle, circular leasing allows multiple drivers to benefit from the same vehicle. This maximizes resource use and reduces overall demand.

Proactive maintenance

Regular servicing to ensure longevity.

With frequent and thorough maintenance, vehicles stay in top condition for longer, delaying the need for replacement and minimizing environmental impact.

Systematic reconditioning

Refurbished after each lease cycle.

Every car goes through a full check-up and refresh before being leased again. This practice ensures safety, quality, and sustainable usage over time.
